CLINICAL TRIAL PROTOCOL: TRIAL-001
Study Title: Phase III Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Novel GLP-1 Receptor Agonist (MF-2847) in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

Protocol Number: TRIAL-001
Sponsor: MedFlow Clinical Research Institute
Principal Investigator: Dr. Rebecca Torres
Study Phase: Phase III

1. STUDY OBJECTIVES
Primary Objective: To evaluate the efficacy of MF-2847 compared to placebo in reducing HbA1c levels in patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus over 52 weeks.
Secondary Objectives: To assess the safety profile of MF-2847, including cardiovascular outcomes and renal function.

2. INCLUSION CRITERIA
- Age 18-75 years
- Confirmed diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus for at least 6 months
- HbA1c between 7.0% and 10.5% at screening
- BMI between 25 and 45 kg/m²
- Stable dose of metformin (≥1000 mg/day) for at least 8 weeks prior to screening
- eGFR ≥ 30 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Written informed consent obtained

3. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
- Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus
- History of diabetic ketoacidosis within the past 6 months
- History of pancreatitis
- Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh Class C)
- History of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2 syndrome
- Pregnant or lactating women
- Known hypersensitivity to GLP-1 receptor agonists
- Current use of insulin or other injectable diabetes medications
- Uncontrolled hypertension (systolic >180 mmHg or diastolic >110 mmHg)
- NYHA Class III or IV heart failure
- Active malignancy (except non-melanoma skin cancer)
- Participation in another clinical trial within 30 days of screening

4. STUDY DESIGN
This is a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study.
Approximately 1,200 patients will be randomized 1:1 to receive:
- Treatment arm: MF-2847 14mg subcutaneous injection once weekly
- Control arm: Matching placebo subcutaneous injection once weekly

5. STUDY DURATION
- Screening period: 2 weeks
- Treatment period: 52 weeks
- Follow-up period: 4 weeks
- Total study duration per patient: approximately 58 weeks

6. ADVERSE EVENT REPORTING
All adverse events must be reported within 24 hours of awareness.
Serious adverse events (SAEs) must be reported within 24 hours via the electronic SAE reporting system.
SUSAR reporting to regulatory authorities within 7 days (fatal/life-threatening) or 15 days (all other SUSARs).

7. DOSING SCHEDULE
Week 1-4: MF-2847 3.5mg SC once weekly (dose escalation)
Week 5-8: MF-2847 7mg SC once weekly (dose escalation)
Week 9-12: MF-2847 10.5mg SC once weekly (dose escalation)
Week 13-52: MF-2847 14mg SC once weekly (maintenance dose)

8. LABORATORY ASSESSMENTS
HbA1c: Screening, Week 12, 24, 36, 52
Fasting plasma glucose: Every 4 weeks
Lipid panel: Screening, Week 26, 52
Renal function (eGFR, UACR): Screening, Week 12, 26, 52
Hepatic function (ALT, AST, bilirubin): Screening, Week 4, 12, 26, 52
Complete blood count: Screening, Week 12, 26, 52
Calcitonin: Screening, Week 26, 52
