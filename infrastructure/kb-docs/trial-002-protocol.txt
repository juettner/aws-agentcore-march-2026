CLINICAL TRIAL PROTOCOL: TRIAL-002
Study Title: Phase II/III Adaptive Design Study of Anti-PD-L1/VEGF Bispecific Antibody (MF-5120) Combined with Carboplatin in Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

Protocol Number: TRIAL-002
Sponsor: MedFlow Clinical Research Institute
Principal Investigator: Dr. James Wilson
Study Phase: Phase II/III

1. STUDY OBJECTIVES
Primary Objective: To evaluate the overall response rate (ORR) and progression-free survival (PFS) of MF-5120 in combination with carboplatin versus pembrolizumab plus carboplatin in treatment-naive advanced NSCLC patients.
Secondary Objectives: To assess overall survival (OS), duration of response (DOR), and safety profile.

2. INCLUSION CRITERIA
- Age ≥ 18 years
- Histologically or cytologically confirmed Stage IIIB or IV NSCLC
- No prior systemic therapy for advanced/metastatic disease
- PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1% by IHC
- ECOG Performance Status 0-1
- At least one measurable lesion per RECIST v1.1
- Adequate organ function:
  * ANC ≥ 1,500/µL
  * Platelets ≥ 100,000/µL
  * Hemoglobin ≥ 9.0 g/dL
  * Creatinine clearance ≥ 50 mL/min
  * Total bilirubin ≤ 1.5 × ULN
  * AST/ALT ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5 × ULN with liver metastases)
- Life expectancy ≥ 12 weeks
- Written informed consent

3. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
- Known EGFR mutation, ALK rearrangement, or ROS1 fusion
- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic immunosuppression
- Known brain metastases (unless treated and stable for ≥ 4 weeks)
- History of interstitial lung disease or pneumonitis
- Active hepatitis B or C
- HIV-positive with detectable viral load
- History of solid organ transplant
- Systemic corticosteroids (>10 mg/day prednisone equivalent) within 14 days
- Major surgery within 28 days
- Uncontrolled hypertension despite optimal medical management
- History of hemorrhagic or thrombotic events within 6 months
- Proteinuria > 2+ on dipstick

4. SAFETY MONITORING
Given the known class effects of both anti-PD-L1 and anti-VEGF agents, the following adverse events require immediate attention:
- Grade 3+ immune-related adverse events (irAEs)
- Grade 3+ hemorrhagic events
- Grade 3+ hypertension not controlled within 72 hours
- Neutropenia Grade 4 or febrile neutropenia
- Any hepatotoxicity with ALT/AST > 5× ULN
- Proteinuria with nephrotic syndrome

Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) reviews safety data every 6 months.
Independent radiological review committee for tumor assessments.

5. DOSING REGIMEN
MF-5120: 1200mg IV every 3 weeks
Carboplatin: AUC 5 IV every 3 weeks for 4-6 cycles
Pembrolizumab (comparator arm): 200mg IV every 3 weeks

Treatment continues until disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, or up to 35 cycles (approximately 2 years).

6. DOSE MODIFICATIONS FOR NEUTROPENIA
Grade 3 Neutropenia (ANC 500-999/µL): Hold carboplatin, resume at AUC 4 when ANC ≥ 1500
Grade 4 Neutropenia (ANC <500/µL): Hold all treatment, add G-CSF support, resume at reduced doses when ANC ≥ 1500
Febrile Neutropenia: Hospitalize, broad-spectrum antibiotics, hold all treatment until resolved, reduce carboplatin to AUC 4
