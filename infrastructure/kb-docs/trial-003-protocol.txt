CLINICAL TRIAL PROTOCOL: TRIAL-003
Study Title: Phase III Randomized, Double-Blind Study Comparing Novel JAK1-Selective Inhibitor (MF-7890) with Adalimumab in Patients with Moderate-to-Severe Rheumatoid Arthritis

Protocol Number: TRIAL-003
Sponsor: MedFlow Clinical Research Institute
Principal Investigator: Dr. Maria Santos
Study Phase: Phase III

1. STUDY OBJECTIVES
Primary Objective: To demonstrate non-inferiority of MF-7890 compared to adalimumab in achieving ACR50 response at Week 24 in patients with moderate-to-severe RA who have had inadequate response to methotrexate.
Secondary Objectives: To evaluate ACR20/70 responses, DAS28-CRP changes, radiographic progression, patient-reported outcomes, and safety/tolerability.

2. INCLUSION CRITERIA
- Age 18-80 years
- Diagnosis of RA per 2010 ACR/EULAR criteria for at least 6 months
- Active disease: ≥ 6 swollen joints (of 66) and ≥ 6 tender joints (of 68)
- DAS28-CRP ≥ 3.2 at screening
- CRP ≥ 0.5 mg/dL or ESR ≥ 28 mm/hr at screening
- Stable dose of methotrexate (10-25 mg/week) for ≥ 8 weeks prior to screening
- Inadequate response to at least one conventional DMARD
- No prior biologic DMARD or JAK inhibitor use
- Negative tuberculosis test (QuantiFERON or PPD)
- Written informed consent

3. EXCLUSION CRITERIA
- Prior use of any biologic DMARD (including TNF inhibitors, IL-6 inhibitors, T-cell costimulation modulators, B-cell depleting agents)
- Prior use of any JAK inhibitor
- Active or chronic infection
- History of serious infection requiring hospitalization within 6 months
- Herpes zoster within 6 months
- History of lymphoproliferative disorder or malignancy (except treated basal cell carcinoma)
- Hemoglobin < 9.0 g/dL
- ANC < 1,000/µL
- Lymphocyte count < 500/µL
- Platelet count < 100,000/µL
- eGFR < 40 mL/min
- Hepatic impairment (ALT/AST > 2× ULN)
- History of DVT/PE or current anticoagulant use
- Cardiovascular event within 6 months

4. STUDY ARMS
Arm A: MF-7890 15mg oral once daily + methotrexate + placebo injection
Arm B: Adalimumab 40mg SC every 2 weeks + methotrexate + placebo oral tablet
Randomization: 1:1, stratified by region, prior DMARD use, and baseline DAS28-CRP

5. SAFETY MONITORING - SPECIAL INTEREST
- Herpes zoster reactivation (known JAK inhibitor class effect)
- Thromboembolic events (DVT, PE, arterial thrombosis)
- Major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE)
- Malignancy (including lymphoma)
- Hepatotoxicity
- Cytopenias (neutropenia, lymphopenia, anemia)
- GI perforations
- Elevated CPK

6. LABORATORY MONITORING SCHEDULE
CBC with differential: Screening, Week 2, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, then every 12 weeks
Liver function (ALT, AST): Screening, Week 4, 8, 12, then every 12 weeks
Lipid panel: Screening, Week 8, 24, then every 24 weeks
CPK: Screening, Week 12, 24
Inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR): Every 4 weeks
Hepatitis B/C reactivation monitoring: Every 12 weeks in previously exposed patients

7. ADVERSE EVENT MANAGEMENT
Injection site reactions (adalimumab arm): Manage with ice packs and antihistamines; document severity and duration
Elevated ALT/AST > 3× ULN: Hold study drug until < 2× ULN, resume at same dose; discontinue if recurs > 5× ULN
ANC < 500/µL: Discontinue study drug permanently
Lymphocytes < 500/µL: Hold study drug until > 750/µL, resume if returns within 4 weeks
Hemoglobin < 8.0 g/dL: Hold study drug until > 9.0 g/dL, resume at same dose
Herpes zoster: Hold study drug, treat with antivirals, resume after resolution
Suspected DVT/PE: Discontinue study drug, initiate anticoagulation as clinically indicated
